WORLD AIR QUALITY DATA

Restrictions on travel and the closure of businesses and major industries imposed to halt the spread of the coronavirus have resulted in unprecended reductions in air pollution around the world. Analysis of air quality data from the World Air Quality Index project has shown reductions in deadly particulate matter (PM 2.5) by 27% from January 2020 to April 2020. The pollutant, known as PM 2.5, is smaller than 2.5 micrometers in diameter and is considered dangerous as it can lodge deep into the lungs and pass into other organs and the bloodstream, causing serious health risks. The World Air Quality Index project tprovides regularly updated air quality data for about 380 major cities in the world from January until present. Analysis of air quality data is from 1 January until 2 May 2020.

COVID-19: Positive Cases vs. Deaths


This website displays findings from analysis conducted on global weather data. The data was pulled on January 24, 2020 from over 500 randomly chosen cities across the world through the OpenWeatherMap API.

After assembling the dataset, various factors of weather were analyzed against the latitude of the cities in order to understand how latitude or location of cities relates to factors including temperature, cloudiness, wind speed and humidity.

This site provides the source data and visualizations created as part of the analysis as well as explanations and descriptions of trends and correlations observed.
